# 1st revolution

The first industrial revolution led to iron coal and steam technology， the expansion of railroads and the mass production of textiles.

![](images/5e83f7310ea91e8b2535767253c7133f8c398816d5e239b7796f27e0dbfd8082.jpg)

# 2nd revolution

Increase steel production, introduction of electric power and use of petroleum. Inventions of cars, airplanes, electric-powered trolleyes and subway trains.

![](images/bacf0410fc020e67104cb7b6ad8de77b25a4916c199c25b8e721e5e163df5aa9.jpg)

# 3rd revolution

Introduction of nuclear energy. Rise of electronincs (trannsistor and microprocessor), telecommunication and computers. This is the era of high-level automation thanks to two major inventions: PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers) and robots.

![](images/23964faa75a250cafa2857835fa3691accbcd511a359249884cd30b308061776.jpg)

# 4th revolution

Automation of manufacturing using modern smart technology. Large-scale M2M communication and loT are integrated for increased automation, communication and selfmonitoring, and production of smart machines that can analyze and diagnose issues without the need for human intervention.

# Cloud

Cloud computing is a model for enabling ubiquitous,convenient, on-demand network access to a shared pool of configurable computing resources that can be rapidly provisioned and released with minimal management effort or service provider interaction.*

In other word...

The delivery of computing services (servers, storage, databases, networking, software and more) over the Internet ("the cloud").

*"The NIST Definition of Cloud Computing", Special Publication 800-145, U.S. Department of Commerce

![](images/205bdaaa004357d74a6fa039a1ecd731e99da11907737ea9b8706013fe21c48c.jpg)

# Cloud Why?

# IT staff cost saving

Companies become less dependent on on-site IT professionals for troubleshooting.

# Scalability/Flexibility

Companies can expand and decrease their computing capabilities at any time depending on their needs.

# Mobile Accessible

Mobile workers have increased productivity due to systems accessible in an infrastructure available from anywhere.

# Hardware cost saving

Companies can reduce their capital expenditures by only pay for the computing services that they need (pay as you go).

# Reliability

Services using multiple redundant sites can support business continuity and disaster recovery.

# Maintenance
